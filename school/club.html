What is Police Boys and Girls Club?

      The Police Boys and Girls Club is an interactive volunteering opportunity for 9 to 17-year-old children. Led by school staff and supported by police, children will learn about policing issues, staying safe and undertake some volunteering activity in support of a local policing priority. As well as benefitting the community, research highlights the benefits for the personal development of young people involved as well the positive benefits this can have on their overall trust and confidence in the police.

Aim of the Club :

To bring Police & Public under one umbrella, protecting right image among the public.

!.........*****♠ ♣ ♠*****.........!

Objectives:

  • To remove the prejudice that public have towards the police and to make them understand the realities of the work of police personnel.
  • To promote crime awareness among the people and to motivate them to assist the police in crime prevention activities.

     In an effort to create awareness among school students on the social evil of child marriage, POCSO Act and safe usage of social media, the law enforcers have started Police Boys and Girls Club.

  • Originally, the Police Boys and Girls clubs for school students were started in 2003 in Tamil Nadu as a crime prevention strategy.

  • The city police are forming police boys and girls club to prevent children from socially and economically backward areas from falling into bad ways.

  • This club will have a select group of 30 students who would be sensitised by a team of visiting trainers
